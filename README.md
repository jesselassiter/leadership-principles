# Technology Leadership Principles  
### Jesse Lassiter

Executive leadership principles developed through enterprise IT modernization, engineering management, and large-scale platform delivery initiatives.

This repository outlines core leadership principles developed through 13+ years of experience across IT operations, engineering management, enterprise platform delivery, and product strategy.

These principles guide modernization initiatives, operational excellence efforts, and the development of high-performing technical teams.

---

## 1. Align Technology With Business Outcomes

Technology initiatives must connect directly to measurable business value. Roadmaps, modernization efforts, and platform investments should clearly support reliability, scalability, risk reduction, and long-term growth.

---

## 2. Prioritize Reliability and Resilience

Enterprise systems must be designed with fault tolerance, observability, and recoverability in mind. Strong reliability engineering practices reduce operational overhead and strengthen organizational confidence.

---

## 3. Build Empowered, Accountable Teams

High-performing teams operate with clarity, ownership, and psychological safety. Leadership is about enabling others to make sound decisions while maintaining strategic alignment.

---

## 4. Modernize With Intent

Modernization should be purposeful, not reactive. Effective transformation balances innovation with operational continuity and disciplined execution.

---

## 5. Simplify to Scale

Complex systems and workflows create fragility. Simplification improves maintainability, reduces risk, and enables scalable growth.

---

## 6. Lead Through Clarity and Communication

Clear expectations, transparent decision-making, and strong cross-functional collaboration drive sustainable execution across distributed teams.

---

## 7. Continuous Improvement Over Perfection

Mature organizations prioritize incremental improvement. Structured feedback loops and operational reviews drive long-term excellence.
